Frequently asked

Quick answers. Full FAQ here, or email us if you don't see your question.

What plugin formats does Plugr support?

VST3, Audio Unit (AU), VST2, AAX, and CLAP. It also catalogues your Mac applications (DAWs, mastering apps, license managers, instrument standalones) so it's truly "everything audio-related on this Mac".

Does Plugr work on Windows?

Not yet. Plugr is currently macOS-only (macOS 12 Monterey or later, Apple Silicon and Intel both). Windows support is on the long-term roadmap but not committed.

How accurate is the update checking?

For the hundreds of developers Plugr knows about — most of the big names you use every day — very accurate. For developers it doesn't recognize yet, Plugr makes its best guess and lets you confirm in one click, then remembers for next time.

A few developers (Plugin Alliance, Native Instruments, UAD, Waves) handle updates through their own installer apps. Plugr knows about those too, and points you to the right place to grab the update.

Will Plugr delete my plugins?

Only when you explicitly tell it to. "Move to Trash" is reversible (files go to your macOS Trash, not deleted permanently) and asks for confirmation. Everything else — categories, tags, favorites — is metadata that lives in Plugr's local cache and never touches your actual plugin files.

What's the difference between subscription and lifetime?

Subscription gives you all features as long as you're subscribed; cancel and the app drops to a read-only view of your existing scan data. Lifetime is a one-time payment for all features available at the time of purchase, plus all updates to those features forever. We may sunset the Lifetime tier at some point — anyone who bought one keeps it.

Does Plugr send my data anywhere?

Your library, projects, tags, favorites and notes stay on your Mac. Plugr only reaches out to developer websites to check for updates and to the deal sites to pull in sales — it never uploads your library anywhere.

If you turn on iCloud sync, your data syncs between your own Macs through Apple's iCloud, not our servers. Full details in the privacy policy.

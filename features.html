Features

Everything Plugr can do

A guided tour of every feature — from the first scan to the deepest corners of your library.

Library

Every plugin and app on your Mac, in one place

Plugr scans the standard plugin folders on your Mac and gives you a single beautiful view of everything you have — VST2, VST3, AU, AAX, CLAP, and the rest of your apps too.

  • Software automatically sorted into instruments, effects, and applications
  • See file size, version, and developer at a glance
  • Switch between a grid of cards or a sortable, resizable table view
  • Filter by format, category, developer, update status, tags, and favorites
  • Multi-format aware — set a category, tag, or update source on one format and the rest of the family (VST3, VST2, AU, AAX, CLAP) inherit it
  • Catches the rest of your apps too — organize everything in one place and never miss an update
Tags & favorites

Make sense of the chaos, your way

Tag plugins however makes sense to you — "vocal chain", "live set", "mastering" — and star the heavy hitters so they're never more than a click away.

  • Custom tags with autocomplete
  • One-click favorites that sit at the top of every view
  • Hide any apps or plugins you don't want to see — they stay out of your way until you want them back
  • Add personal notes to any plugin (presets you love, settings to remember)
  • Reorganize the sidebar by dragging sections around
Updates

Never miss an update again

Plugr keeps tabs on hundreds of plugin developers and tells you the moment a new version comes out — so you'll never wonder again whether your favorite synth has an update waiting or where to get it.

  • Works with hundreds of major developers right out of the box (FabFilter, Soundtoys, Native Instruments, iZotope, Valhalla, UAD, Plugin Alliance, Cableguys, Xfer Records, and many more)
  • Spots big version jumps and flags them with a "Major upgrade" badge so you know which updates might be paid
  • Check your entire library at once with a single click
  • For developers Plugr doesn't recognize yet, the "Find update source" flow lets you either auto-search the developer's site or enter the page manually — and saved sources can be edited or removed later
  • Teach Plugr a source for one plugin and it'll offer to mirror that pattern to every other plugin from the same developer — set up update tracking for a whole catalog with one click
  • Opt in to the community update-source feed: working sources users submit flow back into a shared pool that everyone benefits from, so Plugr keeps getting better every day
Companion Apps

Every developer's update manager in one place

A lot of your library lives behind update managers — Native Access, Waves Central, Splice, Creative Cloud, Plugin Alliance Installer, etc. Plugr puts all of them on one screen so you don't have to dig through Launchpad.

  • A card grid of every update manager on your Mac, opened with a single click
  • See how many apps or plugins each update manager manages, and how many have updates available
  • One click to launch any of them — Native Access, Waves Central, Splice, Plugin Alliance Installer, and more
  • Sort the grid however you like — by name, by plugins managed, or by what has updates pending
Projects

Discover what you actually reach for

Point Plugr at your project folders and it'll show you your library the way you really use it — by what actually shows up in your sessions

  • Works with Ableton Live, Logic Pro, and FL Studio sessions
  • Three charts at the top — most-used plugins, category mix, developers you rely on — each one clickable to filter the list below
  • Auto-detects tempo and project key from every session, so you can filter by a tempo range or sort by key
  • Shows the DAW version each project was last saved in (Ableton Live and FL Studio)
  • Per-project ratings (A–F), customizable workflow statuses ("Rough Concept", "In Progress", "Needs Mixing", "Released"), tags, and notes
  • Bounces auto-discovered from your project folder and played inline allow you to preview and compare different versions of a project without opening your DAW
  • Drag any audio file onto a project to attach it as a bounce; drag a single project file onto Plugr to scan just that one
  • Jump from any project straight into your DAW, or from any plugin to every session you've used it in
Deals

Smart deal hunting, built right in

Plugr keeps an eye on the major plugin sale sites and brings everything into one tidy feed. Smart filters help you focus on what matters to you.

  • Pulls in deals from trusted vendors like Plugin Boutique, Audio Plugin Deals, and more
  • Filter by developers you trust, or just browse everything on sale today
  • See instantly which deals are upgrades to plugins you already own
  • View prices in your local currency — USD, EUR, GBP, JPY
  • Wishlist deals to come back to, dismiss the ones that aren't for you
  • Sort by discount, by price, or by what's newest
Deal Alerts

Never miss a sale on the plugins you actually want

Tell Plugr what to watch — specific plugins, whole developers, or your own custom keywords — and it pings you on macOS the moment a matching sale shows up. Works even for plugins you don't own yet.

  • One-click bell icons next to every plugin, developer name, and deal card — turn watches on and off from anywhere in the app
  • Watch by plugin (e.g. "Melodyne 5"), by developer (e.g. "Plugin Alliance"), or by custom keywords (e.g. "uvi falcon", "celemony") — perfect for plugins Plugr doesn't track natively
  • Native macOS notifications when matches land, with smart 24-hour dedupe so you're never blasted with the same alert twice
  • "N new" badge on the Deals tab so you know exactly when there's fresh stuff to look at
  • "My Deal Alerts…" management modal lists every watch with quick pause/delete controls
  • Optional menu-bar mode keeps Plugr alive in the background so alerts keep firing with the main window closed — pair with launch-at-login for fully hands-off deal monitoring
Tools

The little tools you needed yesterday

The tap tempo, delay calculator, and key picker you've been Googling for years — built right into Plugr so you don't have to leave your session to grab them.

  • Tap tempo — find the BPM of anything by tapping along, or with the spacebar
  • BPM ↔ delay calculator — convert between any BPM and millisecond delay times for any note value
  • Camelot wheel — find harmonically compatible keys for mixing or remixing
  • Always available, always at the same place — no more juggling browser tabs

And everything else you'd hope for

The smaller features that add up to a much better experience.

Studio palette themes

Eleven themes built in — three system defaults plus eight studio-inspired palettes to match your vibe.

Bulk editing

Clean up your library fast — rename, retag, or organize hundreds of plugins at once

iCloud sync

Keep your tags, favorites, project library, and update sources synced between your Macs. (Optional)

Export your library

Take your entire library out as a spreadsheet anytime — useful for backup, insurance documentation, or just peace of mind.

Backup & restore

Save your entire Plugr setup — tags, favorites, notes, custom sources — as a single file. Restore it on a new Mac in seconds.

Multi-format aware

Tag, categorize, or set an update source on one format and every other format of the same plugin (VST3, VST2, AU, AAX, CLAP) inherits it automatically.

Duplicate detection

Spot older copies of the same plugin sitting alongside newer versions — perfect for cleaning out years of accumulated crap.

Fast search

Find any plugin by name, developer, or category as you type. Works even on huge libraries with thousands of plugins.

Custom categories

Add your own categories if "Effect" and "Instrument" aren't specific enough — "Mastering Suite", "Live FX", whatever you need.

Right-click for quick actions

Right-click any plugin (or a multi-selection) for a contextual menu — favorite, hide, tag, change category, delete to Trash. The fastest way to clean up a list.

Undo for bulk operations

Apply an update source to a whole developer's catalog, retag two hundred plugins, or mass-remove sources you no longer want — one click of Undo puts everything back. No fear of mass actions.

Menu bar mode

Optionally run Plugr in the macOS menu bar so deal-alert notifications fire even when the main window is closed. Combine with launch-at-login for a fully hands-off setup.
